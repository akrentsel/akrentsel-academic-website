Motivation

Despite significant efforts, modern WANs remain vulnerable to failure, with complex outages persisting over time. Our work aims to address this challenge by simplifying the network architecture.

Figure 1: Historical outage frequency in our WAN. Outages are classified internally based on several factors, including perceived user impact, duration, and blast radius (single or multi-region).

From Centralized to Decentralized SDN

We propose a transition from centralized SDN to a decentralized architecture where the controller functionality is replicated and moved onto the routers.

Figure 2: Animation showing the transition from centralized SDN to decentralized SDN.

Current SDN WAN Architecture

Current SDN WAN architectures introduce significant complexity, with multiple components and dependencies that can lead to failures.

Figure 3: Conceptual diagram of a typical large-scale WAN architecture deployment, with external dependencies shown in green and on-box dependencies shown in blue.

Our Approach: Decentralized SDN (dSDN)

We propose dSDN, a novel architecture that moves SDN control logic to run within routers, eliminating the control plane's reliance on external infrastructure and restoring fate-sharing between control and data planes.

Figure 4: Diagram of dSDN's system architecture.

Key Components of dSDN

  1. Building a global view through Node State Updates
  2. Each node computes all paths locally
  3. Forwarding follows source routes

Performance Evaluation

We evaluated dSDN against centralized SDN (cSDN) and RSVP-TE in terms of convergence time and impact on traffic.

Figure 8: Individual components of convergence time in B4 for cSDN vs. dSDN.
Figure 9: Total convergence time in B2 for RSVP-TE vs. dSDN.
Figure 10: Bad Seconds distribution for cSDN and dSDN, for different traffic classes.

Key Results

  • dSDN achieves up to 120-150x faster convergence times than cSDN
  • dSDN sees significantly lower impact (measured in bad seconds) for all traffic priorities
  • dSDN maintains its performance advantage even under high churn rates
  • dSDN is practical to implement on current router hardware and scales well with network growth

Conclusion

dSDN represents a new point in the design space that achieves the best of both centralized SDN and distributed protocols. It maintains the benefits of SDN while significantly simplifying the control plane infrastructure and improving convergence performance.
